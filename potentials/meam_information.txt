Dimer (zero at long distances, which is correct): Li,Na,C,Ge,Si,Al,Cu,Ni,Rh,Ag,Ir,Pt
Note: This is because the background electron density is subjected to a Taylor expansion (which deals with the ratio of partial electron densities).
Note: The bond distances of the dimers are in good agreement between MEAM and DFT. However, Mo, Cr, and C differ by 20%.
Note: Using the background electron density of each orbital instead of the ratio of the background electron density may be applicable to non-bulk systems (such as dimers). Note that the weighting factor t may become negative, resulting in a negative value in the calculation of the sum.
Note: In bulk, the ratio of the background electron density is almost the same. However, verification is required for the surface.
